**<p style="text-align: center;">At the edge of the world, comes the edge of reason.</p>**

Truzane is a small world, industrious and quiet. Its twin continents huddle close amid an endless expanse of ocean, ancient brothers forever competing in culture, in science, in war. By the modern day, the northern nation of Voorman and the southern Althus live peacefully, if not entirely harmoniously, pushing each other in feats of engineering or scientific discovery. But a chance discovery at the dawn of the decade has engaged them in an all-too-cold war once more.

As understanding of planar geography grew, ancient legends of a third continent proved to be myth no longer, as tentative journeys beyond the known edge of Truzane have discovered a polar landmass never before encountered by man. A scramble has ensued, with expeditions from both continents racing to this hitherto unknown pole—World's End—to claim it for their own, to plant the flag of their nation where no other foot has trodden. Cutting-edge technology and derring-do are amassed in equal parts to make the first polar landfall and reap the glory it entails.

But the pole is not an empty expanse of ice. Something stirs here. Warming climes, the insidious thrum of machinery, and the scent of prey in the air—all this and more is awakening something ancient. Amid the deafening polar winds and the bass crescendo of shearing ice, one question still lingers in the cold air: What goes there?

**What Goes There?** is a draftable premier set using Play Boosters, containing 266 cards (20 mythic rares, 60 rares, 100 uncommons, 81 commons, and 5 basic lands). Bundle purchasers will also obtain a promotional alternate art copy of SOME CARD, while all boosters have a chance of containing the Headliner  version of SOME OTHER CARD.

###Mechanics

<img align="left" width="200" height="353" style="margin:15px" src="https://i.ibb.co/674m2QLJ/booster.png "WGT booster"/>**What Goes There?** features the following mechanics:

**Endeavor:** The rush to World's End is not for the faint of heart, but those who dare, win. Whenever you tap a creature with endeavor, you may put a stun counter on it. If you do, it will trigger its endeavor effect, which varies card to card. It doesn't matter why the card became tapped, so take advantage of things like Vehicles or other tap effects, or simply launch into combat.

**Innovation:** The work we leave behind carries on after we do. Creatures with innovation have a linked spell in their textbox—this is their innovation, and it can be cast from your graveyard as a copy, with the original card being exiled on resolution. For those cards whose innovation is a permanent spell, the copy will enter the battlefield as a token, but the original card will remain in exile.

**Thaw:** Frozen in time, originating before history began, the teqeli remain trapped in the ice of World's End. Cards with thaw can be played face down as a snow land that can tap for colourless mana—this functions exactly like a normal land play, so can only be done once per turn. The face-down lands can be turned up at sorcery speed for their thaw cost, so once you no longer need the source of snow mana, thaw out your ancient starspawn or their eldritch magicks to announce the awakening of the elder ones.

**Landfall:** Returning from *Zendikar*, landfall represents the scramble for territory as expeditions race for the pole. Landfall triggers when a land enters the battlefield, with the effects varying from card to card. Landfall will also see thaw cards being played as lands, as these operate the same as normal land plays—make the most of your sequencing accordingly for maximum value.

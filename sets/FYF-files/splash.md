**<p style="text-align: center;">Choose destiny. Choose fate. Choose wisely.</p>**

Welcome back, traveller, to the land of Yor-Om. A land of adventure, danger, and infinite consequence. Your choices in this endless frontier are many, and yet so few of them will lead to success. Will you pursue the brutal soom warlord Ycct across the wilderness, or challenge the dragon-sage Akathax to a battle of wits? Will you confront the looming spectre of the Zuvas, the dream-weaving archmage; ride to battle against the blood-mad legions of the warbringer Khulut; or fall into Brod's struggle to claim the Black Throne of Yor-Om for himself?

**Yor-Om: Find Your Fate** is a draftable premier set using Play Boosters, containing 271 cards (20 mythic rares, 60 rares, 100 uncommons, 81 commons, 5 basic-rarity dual lands, and 5 basic lands). Bundle purchasers will also obtain a promotional alternate art copy of King of the Black Throne, while all boosters have a chance of containing the Headliner rulebook frame version of Crossroads of Fate.

###Mechanics

<img align="left" width="200" height="353" style="margin:15px" src="https://i.ibb.co/FjJhdw1/booster.png "FYF booster"/>In addition to its focus on three-colour "wedges", **Yor-Om: Find Your Fate** features the following mechanics:

**Experience:** As you wind your way through the lands of Yor-Om, you may need to test your mettle, your wits, or your strength in order to proceed. Will you fail ignominiously or thrust forward in victory? For a creature to make an experience check, you have the choice to exile a card from your graveyard with mana value greater than that creature's power. If you can't, nothing happens. If you can, and choose to, then put a +1/+1 counter on the creature to represent its improved prowess.

**Narrative:** Your choices will write your story page by page. Narrative is an ability word representing effects which trigger whenever you cast a split spell (a spell represented by two halves of a card, such as Catch the Mist // Catch the Myth) or a modal spell (a spell whose effects ask you to choose options from a bulleted list). Be careful, because permanents with modal "enters the battlefield" triggers will not be affected here—by the time they're asking for your choice they're no longer spells.

**Impending:** Returning from *Duskmourn: House of Horror*, impending spells foreshadow something yet to come—a taste of the adventures that await in the lands of Yor-Om. When a creature spell is cast for its impending cost, it enters the battlefield with a predetermined number of time counters, and loses one each turn. Until the last is gone, it isn't a creature—merely a reminder of what awaits.

**Memory:** Sometimes the echo of great legends lives on in those who come afterwards. Creatures with memory can be cast in one of two ways—their default mode is as a legendary creature, but they can also be cast with different base power and toughness as a nonlegendary creature for their memory cost. They still retain the same name and mana cost, but their abilities may differ slightly due to no longer being legendary.

**Spree:** Returning from *Outlaws of Thunder Junction*, spree is a mechanic found on instant and sorcery spells, allowing you to build your own modal spell from a range of options. When casting a spell with spree, choose one or more of its additional costs to add those stated effects to the spell. Although not bulleted like a traditional modal spell, spree spells are still considered modal for narrative triggers.

#Block Planning

---

Shortly after the introduction of Play Boosters and the associated restricting of premier sets to accommodate them, I decided to follow suit with an overall restructuring of my approach to set design. Inspired by the canon example of *Zendikar Rising*, *Kaldheim*, and *Strixhaven: School of Mages*, which had been loosely tied together with the theme of modal double-faced cards. Similarly, Grappleverse blocks are held together with broad mechanical ties, rather than narrative ones, and sets in a block are self-contained despite the presence of a mechanic throughline. Some ties are fairly blunt and direct, others may be slightly more high-concept, but all should be seen as just an additional element of set-dressing to allow contemporary sets to feel like they exist as part of a larger ecosystem.

<img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/SMK-files/icon.png" alt="SMK"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/FSH-files/icon.png" alt="FSH"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/HTL-files/icon.png" alt="HTL"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/JMR-files/icon.png" alt="JMR"/>**An Embattled Block**: Exploring the battle card type, the sets of the Embattled Block each look at the type through a different lens, finding new ways to execute on it instead of the pre-existing Siege template. We can see the high-speed Race type on the nuclear wasteland of [<font color="#004628">*The Big Smoko*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/SMK>), explore battle tokens in the bizarre ruins of [<font color="#004628">*Morstad: City of Shadows*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/FSH>), send lone operatives to claim Operations in [<font color="#004628">*Heartland of the Real*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/HTL>), or take stock of a world where battles have become an everyday way of life in [<font color="#004628">*Jamuraa: The Mirage War*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/JMR>).

<img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/SON-files/icon.png" alt="SON"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/FYF-files/icon.png" alt="FYF"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/APS-files/icon.png" alt="APS"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/URZ-files/icon.png" alt="URZ"/>**An Historic Block**: Taking the "historic" batching mechanic to its logical conclusion, the Historic Block is itself a batch of individual concepts tied together using the historic theme of artifacts, legends, and Sagas. Journey beyond the Dracula Nebula with Captain Flash Byrn in the artifact-laden [<font color="#004628">*Return of the Revenge of the Son of the Terrorscope*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/SON>), claim your own path to glory in the legendary-driven [<font color="#004628">*Yor-Om: Find Your Fate*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/FYF>), recount ancient and holy lore in the Saga-focused [<font color="#004628">*The Apostate of Kushab*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/APS>), or take on the entire batch at once as we journey through the history of the Weatherlight Saga in [<font color="#004628">*Urza's Chronicle*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/URZ>).

<img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/WGT-files/icon.png" alt="WGT"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/GNT-files/icon.png" alt="GNT"/><img align="left" width="30" height="30" style="margin:2px" src="https://grapplex.github.io/sets/MRC-files/icon.png" alt="MRC"/>**A Manaclash Block:** Conceptually exploring the non-standard ways that Magic can handle mana, the sets of the Manaclash Block each take a new spin on how to value mana from specific sources and how that can affect multiple play styles. The eldritch polar horror of [<font color="#004628">*What Goes There?*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/WGT>) examines snow mana; colourful kaiju madness in [<font color="#004628">*Gigantic*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/GNT>) places an emphasis on non-land sources of mana; and the mercantile hellscape of [<font color="#004628">*Mercadia: Blood and Gold*</font>](<https://grapplex.github.io/previews/MRC>) puts special focus on the use of Treasures, if you have the coin to spend.

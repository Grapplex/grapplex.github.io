Block Planning

Shortly after the introduction of Play Boosters and the associated restricting of premier sets to accommodate them, I decided to follow suit with an overall restructuring of my approach to set design. Inspired by the canon example of Zendikar Rising, Kaldheim, and Strixhaven: School of Mages, which had been loosely tied together with the theme of modal double-faced cards. Similarly, Grappleverse blocks are held together with broad mechanical ties, rather than narrative ones, and sets in a block are self-contained despite the presence of a mechanic throughline. Some ties are fairly blunt and direct, others may be slightly more high-concept, but all should be seen as just an additional element of set-dressing to allow contemporary sets to feel like they exist as part of a larger ecosystem.

List of blocks

A Golden Block: Built around the age-old concept of multicolour sets, the Golden Block begins with the arc-focused adventure world of Chronicles of Yor-Om, the allied-pair metal factions of Heavy Thunder, the wedge-coloured factions courting the cultist Gleeb in Children of Lesser Gods, and the five-colour chromatic focus of Overlay.

A Double-Faced Block: Playing with different executions of double-faced card technology, the three sets of the Double-Faced Block include the morbid body horror of Skand: Heavy Metal War, the Irish mythology world of Ulaith: Cos Teilo's Feast, and the sinister Italian cinema inspired Profondo Giallo. Sets within the block explore modal and transforming DFCs, including meld, augment, and several executions on traditional transformation.

An Embattled Block: Exploring the battle card type, the sets of the Embattled Block each look at the type through a different lens, finding new ways to execute on it instead of the pre-existing Siege template. We can see the high-speed Race type on the nuclear wasteland of The Big Smoko, explore battle tokens in the bizarre ruins of Morstad: City of Shadows, send lone operatives to claim Operations in Heartland of the Real, or take stock of a world where battles have become an everyday way of life in Jamuraa: The Mirage War.

An Historic Block: Taking the "historic" batching mechanic to its logical conclusion, the Historic Block is itself a batch of individual concepts tied together using the historic theme of artifacts, legends, and Sagas. Journey beyond the Dracula Nebula with Captain Flash Byrn in the artifact-laden Return of the Revenge of the Son of the Terrorscope, claim your own path to glory in the legendary-driven Yor-Om: Find Your Fate, recount ancient and holy lore in the Saga-focused The Apostate of Kushab, or take on the entire batch at once as we journey through the history of the Weatherlight Saga in Urza's Chronicle.

A Manaclash Block: Conceptually exploring the non-standard ways that Magic can handle mana, the sets of the Manaclash Block each take a new spin on how to value mana from specific sources and how that can affect multiple play styles. Colourful kaiju madness in Gigantic places an emphasis on non-land sources of mana; the eldritch polar horror of What Goes There? examines snow mana; and the mercantile hellscape of Mercadia: Blood and Gold puts special focus on the use of Treasures, if you have the coin to spend.
